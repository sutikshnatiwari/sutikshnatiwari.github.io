Data AnalyticsEconomicsQM716
Locked in Place — Racial Income Gaps & Real Earnings in the U.S.
Led data analysis for a 5-person team examining racial income and wealth inequality across the U.S. from 2002–2024, using U.S. Census Bureau and Bureau of Labor Statistics data. Key findings: Black household median income has remained ~35% below White households for two decades despite multiple economic cycles; Asian households now earn ~40% above White medians; cumulative inflation of 77% far outpaced median income growth of just 20%, eroding real purchasing power by roughly one-third for Black and White households. Analyzed wealth accumulation by age and education, income skewness within racial groups, and correlation patterns (Black–national: 0.967; inflation–income: 0.08). Sunny led dataset cleaning and organization from the Census Bureau's 2025 income report, and co-built all data visualizations.
